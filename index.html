Primary Research

Computational Analysis of Marital Interactions

NSFC General Program • Oct 2022 - Present • Primary Student Researcher

Leading a comprehensive computational analysis of spousal interactions using state-of-the-art natural language processing methodologies, including Linguistic Inquiry and Word Count (LIWC) sentiment analysis, Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) topic modeling, and fine-tuned transformer-based language models (BERT, RoBERTa). This interdisciplinary research bridges psychological theory with artificial intelligence to decode complex relationship dynamics and predict marital satisfaction trajectories.

Key Achievements:

  • Systematically analyzed 720+ spousal interaction transcripts achieving 79% inter-rater coding consistency (Cohen's κ = 0.76)
  • Developed novel multimodal analysis pipeline integrating textual, acoustic, and behavioral data streams with automated quality control
  • Achieved significant predictive accuracy for relationship satisfaction outcomes (R² = 0.68, p < 0.001, cross-validated)
  • First-author manuscript under review at Journal of Marriage and Family (Impact Factor: 3.9, Q1 in Family Studies)
