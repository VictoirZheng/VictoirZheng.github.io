Research

Research Interests

My primary research interest lies at the intersection of developmental psychology (especially relationship science), computational methods and technology. I am passionate about leveraging advanced computational methods such as Natural Language Processing (NLP), machine learning/deep learning, multimodal analysis, and large language models (LLMs) to investigate spousal and family interaction patterns and dynamics, and their impact on individual mental health and relationship outcomes.
Beyond academic reserach, I'm further fascinated by bridging the gap between theory discovery and real-world impact. I am driven to combinine psychology theories and paradigms with cutting-edge AI-powered techniques, aiming to create accessible and user-friendly tools for public mental regulation and convenient psychotherapy practice.

My key areas of interest include:
  • Relationship Science: Modeling how verbal and non-verbal communication patterns within couples and families predict individual well-being and relationship satisfaction.
  • Social Computation: Developing and applying computational methods (NLP, ML, DL and LLMs) to objectively quantify and analyze the complex, multimodal dynamics of human interaction.
  • Digital Mental Health: Translating computational insights into evidence-based, AI-driven tools and interventions to support mental well-being and enhance psychotherapeutic practice.

Projects

Computational Analysis of Marital Interaction and Relationship Quality

Duration: Oct 2022 - Jun 2025
Role: Primary Student Researcher
Description: This large-scale project investigates how the multimodal dynamics of spousal interaction predict marital quality. As the primary student researcher, I was responsible for the entire computational pipeline. This included applying NLP models like BERT and topic modeling to analyze over 720 conversation transcripts, and using OpenFace (facial action units) and Whisper (speech-to-text) to extract non-verbal cues from a database of over 1,200 video recordings (>1TB). I also developed a semi-automated analysis workflow in R (Shiny) and Mplus to process and visualize the integrated multimodal data.

Large-Scale Analysis of College Student Mental Health in China

Duration: Oct 2022 - Jun 2025
Role: Lead Student Researcher
Description: In this nationwide project, I led the analysis of mental health data from over 5 million Chinese college students. My primary contribution was developing and maintaining a fully reproducible R and Python pipeline (>6,000 lines of code) to automate data cleaning, psychometric analysis of the CSMHSS scale, and visualization of national mental health trends. I annually co-authored and presented policy briefs for the Ministry of Education of China, contributing directly to national mental health assessment strategies.

Neural Correlates of Marital Interaction in Newlyweds

Duration: Jan 2020 - Jun 2025
Role: Primary Student Researcher
Description: This project explored the neural basis of marital interaction using fNIRS hyperscanning with over 50 newlywed couples. My key responsibility was building an end-to-end video analysis pipeline using Python (TensorFlow) and C++. The pipeline automated the pre-processing of interaction footage and performed facial expression recognition, speech-to-text transcription, and interaction pattern coding. I also conducted the fNIRS experiments and developed an automated system to provide customized feedback reports to participating couples.

Computational Modeling of Emotional and Semantic Spaces

Duration: Jun 2020 - Jun 2022
Role: Core Team Member
Description: As a core team member on these foundational undergraduate projects, I explored how individual lexical-semantic structures relate to emotional well-being. My role involved programming and deploying behavioral experiments in MATLAB and PsychoPy to assess participants' semantic spaces. I then applied machine learning models, including Support Vector Machine (SVM) and Relevance Vector Regression (RVR), to analyze the data and successfully predict depression and anxiety scores from semantic metrics.
